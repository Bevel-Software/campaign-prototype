# Demo Script — AI Campaign Generator for Japan Expansion

**Duration**: ~5 minutes
**Audience**: Client (EGYM Wellpass stakeholder)
**Tone**: Collaborative, hypothesis-driven — we're showing a first draft, not a finished product

---

## Opening (30s)

> "Hey — so after our conversations around the Japan expansion, we put together a walkable prototype of how we think an AI-assisted campaign planning workflow could work for Wellpass.
>
> This is a **first guess** at what the system could look like. We probably got some things wrong, there might be steps missing, data sources we haven't considered, or parts that are unnecessary. That's exactly the kind of feedback we're looking for — not just on the details of each step, but on whether the overall flow makes sense.
>
> Let me walk you through it."

---

## Screen 1 — Campaign Planning Chat (2 min)

### Subslide 1: Initial Brief + Past Campaign Data

> "The workflow starts with a conversation. The marketing manager tells the agent the goal — in this case, expanding Wellpass to Japan with two objectives: **10,000 B2C app installs** and **50 B2B demo requests** over 8 weeks.
>
> The agent immediately recognizes these need to be **two separate campaigns** — App Installs and Lead Gen — because they have different optimization objectives on the ad platforms.
>
> Then it loads **past campaign data** from DACH. This is a key point: the system needs access to your historical performance data — your campaign exports, spreadsheets, whatever format that lives in. That's what powers the recommendations. Here we can see the DACH results: 63K+ users reached on B2C, 28K+ on B2B, with the strongest engagement in the 25-34 age bracket."

### Subslide 2: Brand Strategy

> "Next, it pulls in the **marketing strategy and brand guidelines** — the 'Freedom to move' positioning, the key messaging pillars around variety of sports, employer-paid benefit, zero hassle. This ensures every creative stays on-brand even as we adapt to a new market."

### Subslide 3: Ad Set Structure

> "Based on the data and strategy, it recommends a structure: **3 B2C ad sets** — broad fitness interests, young professionals 25-34, and a DACH lookalike audience — plus **2 B2B ad sets** targeting HR Directors at enterprise and C-Suite at SMEs."

### Subslide 4: Messaging per Ad Set

> "Then it maps **specific value propositions to each segment**. For B2C, things like 'Fitness Freedom' and 'Refresh After Work' adapted to Japanese work culture. For B2B, data-driven angles around employee retention ROI. These are pulled from the loaded personas and value props, not invented from scratch."

### Subslide 5: Budget & Channels

> "The user sets the budget — ¥2,000,000 over 8 weeks starting May 1st — and the agent routes it across channels. **B2C goes to Meta** (Instagram and Facebook), **B2B goes to LinkedIn**. It recommends Advantage Campaign Budget so Meta can auto-optimize across ad sets."

### Subslide 6: Final Plan

> "It confirms the budget split: ¥1.3M for B2C app installs, ¥700K for B2B lead gen. At this point, the full campaign architecture is locked in."

*Gesture to the context panel on the right:*

> "Throughout this whole conversation, you can see the campaign architecture building up on the right — objectives, target market, ad sets, placements, budget, timeline, and critically, the **connected data sources** that power the recommendations."

---

## Screen 2 — Audience Segments (45s)

> "Before generating creatives, you get a table of all ad sets with full details — the audience, the channel, the value proposition, and targeting parameters.
>
> You can **select or deselect** which ad sets to generate creatives for. And there's a field to add **custom instructions** per ad set — for example, if you know a specific angle works better in a market, you can steer the creative direction here.
>
> For this demo, we selected 4 of the 5 ad sets."

---

## Screen 3 — Generation (15s)

> "The content creation agent takes over. It loads your existing DACH creative library — 47 assets — fetches brand assets, brand guidelines, and channel format specs. This is where all the context comes together to generate the actual ads."

---

## Screen 4 — Generated Creatives (1 min)

> "And here are the generated creatives. Two for B2C Meta, two for B2B LinkedIn.
>
> For the B2C ads, we have Japanese copy — '運動したい？会社が払ってくれる！' ('Want to exercise? Your company pays!') — with the appropriate CTAs for app installs. For B2B, professional LinkedIn posts with case study angles and retention data.
>
> Each creative is tagged with its **tone, format, angle, and objective** so you always know what strategic intent it serves.
>
> And this is where it gets interactive — you can click into any creative and **edit the headline, body text, or CTA directly**. You can hit 'Generate variations' on any field to get alternatives. And if the image doesn't work, you can give feedback and **regenerate** it with a new prompt.
>
> A quick note on the Japanese text — it's not perfect yet in the prototype, but the idea is that the system would generate culturally adapted copy for the target market."

---

## Screen 5 — Export (30s)

> "Once you're happy with the creatives, you export. Everything goes into a structured spreadsheet — campaign, ad set, channel, headline, CTA, status — ready to be uploaded to your ad platform or handed to your media buyer.
>
> So the full workflow: **brief the agent in conversation, review the audience segments, generate and refine creatives, export**. End to end."

---

## Closing (30s)

> "So that's the prototype. Again — this is our **first hypothesis** of what this workflow could look like. What we'd really love your feedback on is:
>
> 1. **Are there steps missing?** Things you'd need to do in your actual workflow that aren't represented here.
> 2. **Are there unnecessary steps?** Things we included that don't add value.
> 3. **What data sources are we missing?** What else would the system need access to?
> 4. **Are there parts where we should go deeper?** If any section feels more critical, we can invest more there.
>
> We're happy to iterate on this based on your input — looking forward to hearing your thoughts."
